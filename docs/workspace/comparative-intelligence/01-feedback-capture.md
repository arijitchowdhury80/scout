# Comparative Intelligence Feedback Capture

Date: 2026-07-09
Source: Private beta WhatsApp feedback

## Feedback Summary

A beta tester said Scout's underlying value is strong, but a non-technical
business user should understand at first glance which problems Scout solves.
They pointed specifically at competitive pricing and product/service research:
if a business is comparing what competitors charge, Scout should help break
that down into useful market information instead of presenting itself only as a
generic scraper.

The tester also suggested tutorial/video guidance so new users can discover the
tool's best use cases.

## Product Interpretation

This is not just a messaging issue. It suggests a new product capability:
comparison-ready market intelligence.

Potential examples:

- A shopper or retail analyst asks for the current price of the same TV across
  Best Buy, Costco, Sam's Club, BJ's, and other retailers.
- A founder launching a new product asks Scout to collect comparable products,
  packaging, claims, price points, and source citations.
- A hotel operator asks Scout to compare nightly rates for nearby hotels in the
  same zip code for a given date.
- A B2B team asks Scout to monitor competitor pricing pages and produce a cited
  change report.

## Important Product Boundary

The consumer shopping example is useful as a demo because it is immediately
understandable. The paid product should be positioned as business-grade
comparative intelligence: pricing research, competitor benchmarking, market
mapping, and recurring monitoring.

## Why It Matters

Current Scout positioning says "give us a URL, get structured data." That is
accurate but still technical. This feature moves Scout toward "ask a market
question, get a cited comparison table," which is much easier for business users
to understand and buy.
